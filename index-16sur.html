Survivalist Info

What is a survivalist and what does a survivalist need?

Millions of people believe that we are living in the "end times," whether it will be manmade or a cyclical force of nature. A survivalist is someone who is looking for a viable solution to survive such an event, including preparing by building structures that will help them to survive, such as a survival retreat, home, or underground shelter. They often prepare for self-defense and self-sufficiency as well, and many have included medical training in their preparedness regimen.

Any good survivalist will tell you there are a few absolutes needed if you want to make it through a disaster. The very first thing you need is a PLACE — land — on which you can build a shelter. Someplace that has access to the other necessities, such as clean air, food, and water.

We've already seen signs in soaring fuel and food prices, and everyone is becoming more focused on protecting their families. According to survivalist and energy watch groups, energy shortages are looming. Many are finding a viable solution by moving to rural areas where they can build their home to be self-sustaining.

 

What does Equestrian Estates at White Hills mean to a survivalist?

Equestrian Estates at White Hills'  five acre ranch lots allow you to prepare for the future by becoming self-contained, should you so desire. A 'hard core' survivalist mentality is not a requirement for you to become self sufficient by growing your own food and raising animals. Electric and telephone lines are available, roads are maintained, and we offer amenities such as water to each parcel, allowing you to personalize how independent your lifestyle will be.

Building your home on a 5-acre ranch lot in Equestrian Estates at White Hills in northwestern Arizona makes it easy to be self-sufficient. The land is at a 3000+ foot elevation, meaning the air is cooler than the southern part of Arizona, and it is cleaner. Water? Just minutes away are Lake Mead and Lake Mohave. Food is readily available by fishing in the Colorado River or either of the lakes, and with a 5-acre plot of land you've got plenty of room for gardening and raising animals.

Survivalist homes are encouraged, whether above or below ground. Self-sufficient and eco-friendly homes are celebrated here. And you don't have to worry about "Stepford Wives" type HOA restrictions. The CC&Rs are realistic and generous. Surrounded by BLM land, Equestrian Estates at White Hills provides the remote setting preferred by survivalists. Remote, but not too remote. Shopping at Boulder City is within a 20 minute drive. Kingman is less than an hour's drive to the southeast, and Las Vegas is approximately 45 minutes away, thanks to the new bypass bridge over the Colorado River.
